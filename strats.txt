Taylor rule (reaction function)

Link: Inflation + slack → policy rate

Use with: CPI/Core CPI/PCE + Unemployment (or GDP) → Fed Decision / Fed Funds ladder

Flag: hot inflation + tight labor, yet near-term cuts priced high

Phillips curve / NAIRU

Link: labor tightness ↔ inflation pressure

Use with: Unemployment ↔ CPI/Core/PCE

Flag: unemployment very low (tight) while inflation priced very low (disinflation), or the reverse

Okun’s law

Link: growth ↔ unemployment

Use with: Real GDP ↔ Unemployment / Payrolls

Flag: strong GDP priced with rising unemployment (or weak GDP with falling unemployment)

Sahm rule

Link: unemployment rise → recession risk

Use with: Unemployment ↔ Recession (Q1/Q2 etc.)

Flag: unemployment jump implied but recession probability stays very low (or vice versa)

Beveridge curve

Link: vacancies ↔ unemployment

Use with: JOLTS/Vacancies ↔ Unemployment

Flag: high vacancies + high unemployment (or low vacancies + low unemployment) without a “shift” story priced

UIP benchmark (rate differentials ↔ FX)

Link: relative rates ↔ expected FX moves

Use with: Fed/BoJ decisions or rate thresholds ↔ USDJPY thresholds

Flag: US–Japan rate differential narrows sharply but USDJPY still priced to rise a lot (or vice versa)

Forward-guidance threshold heuristics (“Evans rule” style)

Link: policy held until inflation/labor thresholds

Use with: Unemployment + Inflation ↔ Fed Decision / Fed Funds ladder

Flag: low inflation + weak labor priced, yet hikes/no-easing priced (or high inflation + tight labor with cuts priced)

Humphrey–Hawkins (dual mandate framing)

Link: Fed reacts to both inflation + employment

Use with: not an equation—use as a “prior” that both inflation and labor should matter for rate pricing

Flag: rate path moves wildly with one side while the other side is priced opposite (helps interpret Taylor/Phillips deviations)
